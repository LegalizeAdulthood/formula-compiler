Binary Decomposition

[]

The Binary Decomposition coloring algorithm uses just two colors from
the gradient. It colors fractals according to the "angle" of the last
value of z from the fractal formula. This results in quite abstract and
elegant images.

The two colors used are at the beginning and at the middle of the
gradient.

There is one parameter that selects between two different flavors of the
same algorithm. Each option creates different patterns. The second
option reproduces the coloring used for many fractals in the classic
Beauty of Fractals book.

It often works well to combine this coloring algorithm with other layers
that contain more different colors. Furthermore, low values (for example
4) for the bail-out parameter of the fractal formula usually give the
best results.

Binary Decomposition is available as a coloring algorithm in
Standard.ucl and as a coloring plug-in in Standard.ulb.
